Lasagna Recipe

Lasagna Classica Italiana

Lasagna is a hearty, layered Italian pasta dish made with sheets of tender pasta, rich tomato sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta, savory ground meat (often beef, pork, or sausage), and plenty of melted cheese.

Each layer is carefully stacked—pasta, sauce, cheese—repeated until the dish is full, then baked until golden and bubbly. The result is a comforting, flavorful casserole with a perfect balance of textures: soft pasta, gooey cheese, and a slightly crisp top layer.

Ingredients
  • For the Meat Sauce (Ragù):
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 carrot, finely chopped
    • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
    • 500 g (1 lb) ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
    • 1 can (800 g / 28 oz) crushed tomatoes
    • 2 tbsp tomato paste
    • 1 tsp dried oregano (or Italian seasoning)
    • Salt and black pepper, to taste
    • Fresh basil or parsley, chopped (optional)
  • For the Cheese Filling:
    • 500 g (2 cups) ricotta cheese (or use béchamel sauce)
    • 1 large egg
    • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
    • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • For the Pasta:
    • 12–15 lasagna noodles (cooked or no-boil)
  • For the Topping:
    • Extra mozzarella and Parmesan for layering and finishing
Steps
  1. Prepare the Meat Sauce (Ragù):
    1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
    2. Sauté onion, garlic, carrot, and celery until softened.
    3. Add ground beef (or beef/pork mix) and cook until browned.
    4. Stir in tomato paste, then add crushed tomatoes, oregano, salt, and pepper.
    5. Simmer on low heat for 25–30 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add fresh basil or parsley at the end if using.
  2. Make the Cheese Filling (or Béchamel):
    1. In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese, egg, Parmesan, and 1 cup mozzarella. Mix until smooth.
    2. (If using béchamel instead: make a roux with butter and flour, whisk in warm milk, and season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg.)
  3. Cook the Pasta:
    1. If using traditional lasagna sheets, cook them in salted boiling water until al dente, then drain.
    2. If using no-boil noodles, skip this step.
  4. Assemble the Lasagna:
    1. Spread a thin layer of ragù on the bottom of a baking dish.
    2. Place a layer of pasta sheets on top.
    3. Spread a layer of cheese mixture (or béchamel).
    4. Add another layer of ragù, then sprinkle mozzarella.
    5. Repeat layers (pasta → cheese → sauce → mozzarella) until the dish is full.
    6. Finish with a final layer of sauce, mozzarella, and Parmesan.
  5. Bake:
    1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. Cover dish with foil (to prevent burning) and bake for 25 minutes.
    3. Remove foil and bake another 20 minutes until the top is golden and bubbly.
  6. Rest and Serve:
    1. Let lasagna rest for 10–15 minutes before cutting (this helps it set).
    2. Slice and enjoy!
